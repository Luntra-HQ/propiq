### LinkedIn Post #4: The Hidden Cost Killing Your ROI

**Hook:** There's a hidden cost in rental properties that most investors miss until it's too late.

**Body:**
It's not the obvious ones - property tax, insurance, mortgage. Everyone knows about those.

It's **capital expenditures (CapEx)** - and it destroys more rookie investors than anything else.

**Real example from last week:**

Investor buys property. Monthly cash flow looks great: +$450/month.

Year 2: Roof needs replacing. $18,000.
Year 3: HVAC dies. $8,500.
Year 4: Water heater, electrical issues. $5,000.

Over 4 years, those "minor" repairs ate $31,500 of profit. Their "$450/month cash flow" was actually -$200/month when you include CapEx.

**Here's what experienced investors do differently:**

They budget **1-2% of property value annually** for CapEx. Always.

$300K property? Set aside $3,000-6,000/year for big-ticket repairs.

"But my property is newer!"

Doesn't matter. Everything eventually breaks. Roofs (20-25 years), HVAC (15-20 years), water heaters (10-15 years), appliances (10-12 years).

**The CapEx reserve test:**
If your cash flow doesn't survive a 2% CapEx budget, you don't actually have positive cash flow. You have a time bomb.

**How to protect yourself:**
1. Budget 1-2% of property value for CapEx
2. Run 5-year projections (when will big items need replacement?)
3. Keep a separate CapEx reserve account
4. Don't raid it for "one-time" expenses

The deals that look best on paper often forget CapEx entirely. The deals that actually make money? They bake it in from day one.

**Why everyone forgets it:**
- "My property is newer!" (doesn't matter, everything eventually breaks)

**The real cost:**
- Roof: $15,000-30,000 (every 20-25 years)

- Water heater: $1,500-3,000 (every 10-15 years)
- Appliances: $3,000-5,000 (every 10-12 years)

**How to budget:**
- Set aside 1-2% of property value annually
- $300K property = $3,000-6,000/year
- Keep in separate savings account
- Never raid it for other expenses

**Real example:**
- Investor: "Great! +$450/month cash flow!"
- Year 2: Roof replacement ($18K)
- Year 3: HVAC dies ($8.5K)
- Actual cash flow: -$200/month when you factor CapEx

**Hidden Cost #2: Vacancy (More Than You Think) (200 words)**

**What it is:**
- Lost rent between tenants
- Not just "tenant moves out + find new tenant"
- Includes turnover time, cleaning, minor repairs, showings

**Why everyone forgets it:**
- "I'll find a tenant right away!" (you won't)
- Using 5% vacancy rate (too optimistic for most markets)
- Not accounting for seasonal variations

**The real cost:**
- Average vacancy: 8-10% in most markets
- $2,000/month rent = $200/month vacancy reserve
- Plus turnover costs: cleaning, paint, carpet, lost rent during showings

**How to budget:**
- Research YOUR market's actual vacancy rate
- Use 8-10% as baseline (adjust up/down based on local data)
- Add $500-1,500 per turnover for cleaning/minor repairs
- Assume 1 turnover every 2-3 years

**Real example:**
- Rent: $2,000/month
- Annual income: $24,000
- 8% vacancy: -$1,920/year (-$160/month)
- 1 turnover per 2 years: -$750/year (-$62/month)
- Real vacancy cost: -$222/month (not the $100 you budgeted)

**What it is:**
- Professional: 10-12% of monthly rent + leasing fees
- Self-managing: Your time (worth more than you think)

**Why everyone forgets it:**
- "I'll manage it myself!" (until you get the 2 AM emergency call)

**The real cost:**

**How to budget:**

**Hidden Cost #4: Property Tax Increases Post-Purchase (150 words)**

**What it is:**
- Many properties are assessed at OLD value
- When you buy, county reassesses at PURCHASE price
- Your taxes jump 20-50%

**Why everyone forgets it:**
- Seller's tax bill shows low amount
- You assume that's what you'll pay (it's not)
- Surprise bill 6-12 months after closing

**How to budget:**
- Call county assessor BEFORE buying
- Ask: "What will taxes be if I buy at $X price?"
- Use THAT number, not seller's current bill
- Budget extra 0.5% for annual increases
